Dear ND-CVRL team,

I'm an independent developer in Türkiye building an MIT-licensed open-source reference implementation of a multi-modal biometric authentication kiosk (iris + fingerprint, fused into one decision). The project is called TANIK; the repository is at <https://github.com/Ayberkone/tanik>. It is not a commercial product or a research paper — it is a working system intended to demonstrate honest engineering practice in the biometrics domain (documented threat model, accurately scoped liveness claims, no invented FAR/FRR numbers).

Adam Czajka kindly confirmed on 25 April that ND-IRIS-0405 is requestable via the formal license process at <https://cvrl.nd.edu/projects/data/>, and pointed me at the CVRL OpenSourceIrisRecognition repository as well — both of which I'm grateful for.

I've now read the license agreement carefully, and I want to be straightforward about my situation before submitting anything: **I am not affiliated with an institution that could sign the agreement on my behalf.** I work in the private sector (full-stack web development) but my employer is not the licensee for this open-source project; it is genuinely a personal weekend effort. The license language is explicit that signatures from students, postdocs, or non-delegated faculty are not accepted, and I want to respect that rather than try to navigate around it.

My question is whether ND-CVRL has any path for unaffiliated independent authors of open-source educational projects, or whether the institutional-licensee requirement is a hard wall in all cases. I would completely understand either answer. If the answer is "find an institutional sponsor," I will pursue that route — and the question becomes whether ND-CVRL has any preference for the kind of institution (a Turkish university research group? a private company with biometric-research interest?).

If the answer is that there is no path for an unaffiliated individual, I will use a more openly-licensed dataset for the project's evaluation phase (CASIA-Iris and UBIRIS.v2 are the alternatives I'm planning to apply to in parallel) and will not pursue ND-IRIS-0405 further.

I'd rather hear "no" plainly than spend weeks trying to construct an institutional fiction.

Thank you for your time, and again for the work CVRL puts into making research-grade iris data available to the community.
